How to Use the Outlier Finder

Our Outlier Finder identifies videos that significantly outperform typical view-to-subscriber ratios in any topic or niche. This powerful tool helps you discover viral content patterns, successful content strategies, and trending topics by analyzing performance metrics across YouTube.

1

Enter Search Query

Type any keyword, topic, or niche you want to analyze. Choose between regular videos or YouTube Shorts depending on the content format you're interested in studying. Our algorithm searches through thousands of videos to find the top performers.

2

Customize Filters

Apply advanced filters to narrow your search by minimum views, video age, channel size, and sorting preferences. Filter by channel subscriber count to find outliers from small creators or established channels. Sort results by view ratio, engagement, or viral score.

3

Analyze Performance Data

Review detailed metrics including view-to-subscriber ratios, engagement rates, and viral scores for each outlier video. Use these insights to understand what makes content go viral and apply these patterns to your own content strategy.

Understanding Outlier Metrics

  • View-to-Subscriber Ratio: Measures how many views a video gets relative to the creator's subscriber count
  • Engagement Rate: Calculates the percentage of viewers who liked, commented, or interacted with the video
  • Viral Score: Comprehensive metric combining view ratio, engagement, and time factors to identify viral content
  • Channel Size Filtering: Discover outliers from micro-influencers, small creators, or established channels
  • Content Format Analysis: Separate analysis for YouTube Shorts versus traditional long-form videos
  • Trend Detection: Identify emerging trends and viral content patterns before they become mainstream

Use outlier analysis to understand what content resonates with audiences, identify successful content strategies, discover trending topics, and find inspiration for your own viral content. This tool is invaluable for content creators, marketers, and researchers studying YouTube trends and viral content patterns.
